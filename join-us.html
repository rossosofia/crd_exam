JOIN US!

Want to join us & learn how to play roller derby?

Join our 12 week Basics program where we teach you how to skate and play roller derby.

Our next basic intake will be in January 2024. Keep an eye out on this page, or follow us on Facebook for the exact date of the Intro Night. Meanwhile - do come by and watch some of our matches and talk to us! We are always happy to chat.

If you have any questions, for example where you can skate indoors in the meantime, drop us a mail at basic@copenhagenrollerderby.com. We're looking forward to hear from and hopefully skate with you!

WHO CAN JOIN?

We encourage everyone over 18, regardless of skill level and gender to come try this amazing sport for themselves! - and yes, you can join, even if you cannot roller skate at all at this point! If you are under 18, you can join our juniors.

WHAT TO BRING

Quad Roller Skates, comfortable sports clothes and a water bottle. You must wear knee pads and elbow pads, wristguards and a helmet (skater). We do have some Roller Derby skates and gear that you can borrow, but we can't promise that we will have enough for everyone. Therefore, we recommend you buy some skates. it's important that the skates don't have a heel (i.e. jam skates). Basic quad skates for Roller Derby or park skating will be fine to start with. If you stick with it, you'll likely upgrade your skates after about a year. Affordable starter Roller Derby skates are Riedell R3, Bont Quadstar, or Chaya Ruby. It can be difficult to source Roller Derby skates in Denmark unfortunately, but you can buy them easily online in Europe via: suckerpunchskateshop.com, stickyskates.se, and rollerderbyhouse.eu Helmets, elbow pads, wrist guards, knee pads and mouth guards can be purchased in Copenhagen at Mills

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Where is the ball?

Actually, roller derby doesn't have a ball. The whole game revolves around skaters racing around a track. Players earn points by skillfully passing opponents during the action-packed bouts.
